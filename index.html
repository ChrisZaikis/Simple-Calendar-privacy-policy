Simple Calendar 2026

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 December 2025

Simple Calendar 2026 is designed to help you manage your time, holidays, and events with minimal data collection and clear privacy controls.

This Privacy Policy explains how Simple Calendar 2026 (“App”, “we”, “us” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you use our mobile and web applications.

By installing or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Data Controller

The data controller for Simple Calendar 2026 is the individual developer and owner of the App (“Developer”). If you have any questions, you can contact the Developer using the details at the end of this document.

The App is developed in the European Union and is intended to comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”) and other applicable data protection laws.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you provide directly

When using the App, you may provide the following information:

  • Calendar events – titles, notes, dates, times, reminders, and any other text you enter into an event.
  • Country and region settings – to display local holidays, name days, and observances.

2.2 Account information (when using cloud sync)

If you choose to sign in and enable cloud synchronization:

  • Google account information – basic profile data such as your Google account ID, email address, and display name, provided via Google Sign-In (OAuth).
  • Cloud calendar data – your events and related metadata stored securely in our cloud database to keep your data synchronized across devices.

2.3 Automatically collected information

To keep the App working correctly and securely, we may process limited technical data, such as:

  • Device type, operating system version, and screen size.
  • Approximate time zone and language settings.
  • Basic error and performance information (for example, failed network requests or crash logs) to debug and improve stability.

We do not collect your precise GPS location, contact list, photos, or other sensitive content through Simple Calendar 2026.

2.4 Local storage and cookies

The App uses local storage technologies (such as localStorage or similar) to:

  • Store your events locally on your device (when not using cloud sync).
  • Remember your selected country, theme, and view settings.
  • Remember if you have already seen in-app messages (for example, New Year celebration).

When you access the web version, session cookies or local storage may be used only to provide essential functionality and security, not for cross-site tracking.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above for the following purposes:

  • Provide core calendar functionality – create, edit, display, and delete your events, holidays, and reminders.
  • Cloud synchronization – securely store and sync your calendar across devices when you choose to sign in.
  • Localization – show the correct holidays and observances for your selected country and year.
  • Security and fraud prevention – protect user accounts, prevent abuse, and ensure the integrity of our services.
  • Improvement of the App – analyze anonymized or aggregated technical information to fix bugs and optimize performance.

We do not sell your personal data and we do not build advertising profiles based on your calendar content.

4. Legal Bases (GDPR)

If you are located in the EU/EEA or another region with similar laws, we rely on the following legal bases to process your personal data:

  • Performance of a contract – to provide the App and its core features that you request.
  • Legitimate interests – to maintain and improve the App, protect security, and prevent abuse, in a way that respects your rights.
  • Consent – when you explicitly choose to sign in with Google or enable cloud sync and similar optional features. You may withdraw your consent at any time by signing out and/or clearing your data.

5. Data Storage and Retention

5.1 On your device

When using Simple Calendar 2026 without signing in, all event data is stored locally on your device. You can delete this data at any time by:

  • Using the “Clear All Events” option inside the App, and/or
  • Uninstalling the App from your device.

5.2 In the cloud (Supabase)

When you sign in and enable cloud sync, your calendar data is stored in our cloud database (Supabase or an equivalent provider). Data is retained for as long as:

  • Your account remains active, or
  • Until you request deletion of your cloud data, or
  • Until we remove inactive accounts after a reasonable period of time.

You can request deletion of your cloud-synced events at any time by using the in-app “Clear All Events” option while signed in, or by contacting us directly.

6. Data Sharing and Transfers

We do not sell or rent your personal data. We only share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers – we use trusted third-party providers to host and operate our services, such as:
    • Supabase – database and authentication infrastructure.
    • Google – Google Sign-In and identity services.
    • Vercel or similar – hosting of the website and documentation.
  • Legal requirements – when required to comply with a valid legal request, court order, or law enforcement investigation, in accordance with applicable law.
  • Business continuity – in the unlikely event of a merger, acquisition, or similar transaction, your data may be transferred to the new owner, subject to the same privacy protections.

Service providers may be located in the EU or in other countries. When data is transferred outside the EU/EEA, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent mechanisms, where required by law.

7. Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) between the App and our servers.
  • Access controls to ensure that only authorized systems and services can access the database.
  • Regular updates and security patches for server and client components.

However, no system can be guaranteed 100% secure. You are responsible for keeping your device and your Google account credentials safe and protected.

8. Children’s Privacy

Simple Calendar 2026 is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required by law in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information without appropriate consent, please contact us so that we can delete the data.

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access – to know what personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification – to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure – to request deletion of your data.
  • Right to restriction – to limit how we process your data.
  • Right to data portability – to receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
